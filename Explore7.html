The Scream is an iconic expressionist painting by Norwegian artist Edvard Munch, created in 1893. The artwork depicts a figure with an agonized expression standing on a bridge, set against a swirling, fiery sky. The vibrant colors and bold, undulating lines convey intense emotion and existential angst. Munch created several versions of this piece, using different media, each symbolizing the universal experience of anxiety and despair. Housed in various collections, including the National Museum of Norway, *The Scream* remains a powerful symbol of human emotion and a landmark of modern art.

Art Movement: Expressionism (1893)
Current location: National Gallery, Oslo
