The Mona Lisa is a renowned portrait by Italian artist Leonardo da Vinci, painted between 1503 and 1506 during the Renaissance. The artwork features a seated woman, believed to be Lisa Gherardini, with an enigmatic smile that has captivated viewers for centuries. Her gaze follows the viewer, creating a sense of connection. The subtle sfumato technique blends soft shadows and light, enhancing the realism and depth of the painting. The serene landscape in the background complements the subject's mysterious allure. Housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris, it is one of the most famous and studied works of art in history.

Art Movement: Renaissance (1503)
Current location: Louvre Museum, Paris
