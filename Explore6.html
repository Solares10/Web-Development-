La Ronda de Noche (The Night Watch) is a famous painting by Dutch artist Rembrandt van Rijn, completed in 1642. This large-scale masterpiece depicts a dynamic group portrait of a militia company led by Captain Frans Banning Cocq and Lieutenant Willem van Ruytenburch. The scene captures the figures in mid-action, with dramatic use of light and shadow (chiaroscuro) to emphasize key individuals and create depth. The vibrant composition breaks traditional group portrait conventions, adding energy and narrative. Displayed in the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam, it is one of Rembrandt's most celebrated works and a hallmark of the Dutch Golden Age.

Art Movement: Baroque (1642)
Current location: Museum of Modern Art, New York
