The Persistence of Memory is a surrealist painting by Spanish artist Salvador Dalí, completed in 1931. The artwork features a dreamlike landscape with melting pocket watches draped over objects, including a tree, a ledge, and a distorted face. The scene explores themes of time, decay, and the fluidity of reality, challenging conventional perceptions of permanence. The barren background and Dalí's precise detail add an eerie, otherworldly quality to the piece. This iconic work, housed in the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York, is a hallmark of surrealism and one of Dalí's most recognized creations.

Art Movement: Surrealism (1931)
Current location: Museum of Modern Art, New York
