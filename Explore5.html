Guernica is a powerful anti-war painting by Spanish artist Pablo Picasso, created in 1937. The mural-sized work depicts the horror and chaos of the bombing of Guernica, a town in Spain, during the Spanish Civil War. Rendered in a monochromatic palette of black, white, and gray, the painting features distorted and anguished figures, including a mother with her dead child, a bull, a horse, and shattered bodies.
