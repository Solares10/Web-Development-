Girl with a Pearl Earring is a renowned painting by Dutch artist Johannes Vermeer, created around 1665. Often called the "Mona Lisa of the North," it portrays a young girl turning toward the viewer, her gaze captivating and intimate. She wears an exotic blue and yellow turban and a luminous pearl earring that serves as the focal point of the composition. Vermeer's masterful use of light and soft brushstrokes enhances the painting's realism and mystery.
