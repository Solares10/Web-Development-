American Gothic is a celebrated painting by American artist Grant Wood, completed in 1930. The artwork depicts a stern farmer holding a pitchfork alongside a woman, presumed to be his daughter or wife, standing before a Gothic Revival-style house. The figures’ rigid expressions and the detailed realism of the setting convey themes of rural American life during the Great Depression. The painting’s blend of satire and reverence for Midwestern culture has made it an enduring symbol of American resilience. It is housed in the Art Institute of Chicago and remains one of the most recognizable works of American art.

Art Movement: Post-Impressionism (1989)
Current location: Museum of Modern Art, New York
